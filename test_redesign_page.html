Data Workloads with Valluu

Revolutionising business execution

A Valluu Data Workload is a series of processes used to utilise or change data from both internal and external data sources in near-real-time. The goal of a Data Workload is to automate the flow of data between various stages of processing, such as data ingestion, cleaning, transformation, and storage.

Leveraging the power of Kubernetes, Valluu can run efficiently in the cloud or on your on-premise systems. Valluu integrates with your existing CI/CD pipeline, so your team can develop and deploy with confidence.
Using Valluu

Endless business execution possibilities

Create
Valluu allows you to create Data Workloads through a drag-and-drop interface to directly tap into and use your data sources.

This approach enables faster development, and it opens up to users who understand the business the ability to make changes without needing to learn how to code or rely upon code-capable data engineers.
Release
Deploying Valluu Data Workloads integrates with your existing CI/CD pipelines.

Use
Executing Data Workloads is made simple through Valluu's ability to integrate with other services out-of-the-box. Execution could be started by API requests, MS Teams interactions, or other event-based triggers.

Once execution is complete, Valluu cleanses its footprint to keep your Kubernetes environment clean. As such, it only consumes resources during execution.
Manage
Your workloads are saved to your Valluu library, which you can share with other users in your organisation to collaborate.

The Valluu dashboard enables usage monitoring and facilitates simple reporting.

Use-case example
Consider an insurance business losing renewals for second-car insurance. The underwriter creates a Data Workload with Valluu to review their pricing and risk data to come up with a new pricing plan.

The Underwriter easily schedules the workload from Microsoft Teams and receives a notification when it has finished running. The results are automatically stored in their library, ready to be applied.
